Emotion Visualization

Making what is visible invisible, this is a question that has appealed to both designers and artists. In the emotion visualization projects, we are dealing with this question, where the invisible corresponds to emotional states of people. Making emotions visible, and maybe tangible, might increase emotional self-awareness of people and ease emotional communication between them.

We are working with AR, thinking that it will become a widely used technology in the future and by using this technology seeing information such as emotional states embedded in our regular vision will become something very common.

I have been implementing prototypes of our ideas on HoloLens, using Unity. Below are two strategies we have been exploring for making emotions visible in AR.

Spatial Representation

Initially we came up with the Emotionscape concept, a hypothetical emotionally responsive space. In this concept, the spatial attributes change to reflect emotional states - either what the user experiencing, what another collocated person's experiencing or one that is selected by the user. Our work has been accepted as poster presentations to VRST'17 and CHI'18 .

Body-attached Representation

While imagining spatial emotion representations, we realized it would also be interesting to visualize the emotional states of people within separately - in fact this could have more use cases and could have a more general applicability. Therefore, we started with a series of exploratory workshops on how people would like to visualize their emotional states and created an emotion visualization design framework for AR based on the outcomes of the workshops and literature. Now, we are evaluating the framework and are planning to prototype our work in HoloLens.

Personal Space

As a part of a fellow student Seyma Tasel's project, I built a personal space creation prototype on HoloLens.
